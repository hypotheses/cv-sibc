Professional Summary

Bhoom Suktitipat is currently an instructor in Biochemistry. He graduated with an M.D. from Faculty of Medicine Siriraj Hospital, Mahidol University in 2003. In the same year, he started working as an instructor at the Department of Biochemistry, Faculty of Medicine Siriraj Hospital. He received his Ph.D. in Epidemiology with a focus in Genetic Epidemiology from the Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health in 2010, and worked as a postdoctoral fellow in Statistical Genetics at the National Human Genome Research Institute, NIH, from 2010-2012. Dr. Suktitipat's research focuses on the development and application of statistical methods to understand the effect of important genetic variations and human diseases.

Research Summary

The focus of my research has been the study of the genetic determinants of human diseases. With the evolving genomics technology, the methods to characterize and identify genes in human variation and diseases have changed over time. I am interested in methodological development and application of methods in genetic epidemiology & statistical genetics. Genetic epidemiology and statistical genetics are two disciplines co-existing with each other similar to epidemiology & biostatistics. The former involves more toward the study design and conception of each study, while the latter involves more towards the analytical side. However, we cannot completely separate the two disciplines from one another, as a study without an analytical process that follow will not consider as a complete research study.

Quantitative Genetics: I have been working primarily on quantitative genetics -- genetics that study primarily traits with continuous distribution. In terms of human disease, my focus has been on QTL related to cardiovascular diseases, such as the genetic determinants of vascular inflammation in coronary artery disease , platelet functions, and CVD related phenotypes. I have been working with the genetics of the eye, from refractive error (such as myopia, hyperopia)

Personalized Medicine: One of my primary focuses is on translational research from genes to human disease. Currently, we are developing a database and algorithm to predict risk of common diseases using genetic profile and information from available published database.
